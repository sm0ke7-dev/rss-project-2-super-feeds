# CLIENT.md — TGA-Compliant Pharmaceutical Content

## Overview
- **Industry**: Pharmaceutical / healthcare (Australian market)
- **Regulatory body**: Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA)
- **Content purpose**: Educational, informational — NEVER promotional

## Compliance Rules (Non-Negotiable)

### What You CAN Do
- Write educational content about health conditions and symptoms
- Report on how media covers treatments (media framing approach)
- Attribute claims to media reports, expert commentary, or patient stories
- Discuss general science behind treatments (e.g., how GLP-1 works in general terms)
- Reference publicly available health information

### What You CANNOT Do
- Promote specific prescription medicines directly to consumers
- Make therapeutic claims about any prescription product
- Encourage self-diagnosis or self-treatment
- Suggest one prescription brand is better than another
- Use language that could be interpreted as medical advice
- Include calls to action that direct consumers toward obtaining prescription medicines

### Required Elements
- **Disclaimer at top**: "This article is for informational purposes only."
- **Disclaimer at bottom**: "Nothing in this article constitutes medical advice. Only a healthcare professional can provide guidance on prescription medicines."
- **Attribution**: Every claim must be attributed (e.g., "according to media reports," "experts quoted by [source] noted that...")
- **Balanced framing**: Present both reported benefits and reported concerns

## Brand Voice
- Informative and balanced
- Factual and evidence-based
- Accessible — Grade 8 readability
- Never promotional or enthusiastic about any specific drug
- Neutral when comparing treatments

## Content Structure
- Inverted triangle: key takeaway first
- Max 3 sentences per paragraph
- Use bold and italic judiciously for readability
- Include pull quotes from attributed sources when they add value
- Formatting elements (bullets, tables) only when they genuinely improve readability

## SEO Approach
- Target informational/educational keywords (e.g., "what is [drug]", "[drug] side effects reported")
- Frame titles around media coverage and public discussion, not medical guidance
- Meta descriptions should reflect educational intent
- Never use commercial-intent keywords that imply purchasing

## Banned Words (Additions to Global List)
- "Best" (when referring to prescription treatments)
- "Cure"
- "Miracle"
- "Guaranteed"
- "You should take"
- "We recommend"
- Any language implying a specific treatment outcome

## Client-Specific Lessons
- Media framing approach is the safest strategy: "according to reports" rather than stating as fact
- Always triple-check that no sentence reads as a direct recommendation
- When in doubt, attribute — over-attribution is always safer than under-attribution
