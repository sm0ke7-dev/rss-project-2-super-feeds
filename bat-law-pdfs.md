# Bat Laws & Regulations — Official PDF Reference List

Research compiled for AAAC Wildlife Removal service area states.
Sources prioritized: .gov and .edu domains only. No competitor company sources included.

## Federal / Multi-State

---

### 1. Endangered Species Act Status for Northern Long-Eared Bat (Final Rule 2022)
- **URL:** https://www.fws.gov/sites/default/files/documents/Final%20Rule%202023-01656_3742527.pdf
- **Jurisdiction:** All states (federal, affects TN, NC, IN, OH, MO, and others in range)
- **Description:** Final rule reclassifying the northern long-eared bat from threatened to endangered under the ESA, effective March 31, 2023. Nullifies the previous section 4(d) rule and imposes full ESA take prohibitions.
- **Source:** U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service (USFWS)

---

### 2. Federal Register — Endangered Species Status for Northern Long-Eared Bat (Nov 30, 2022)
- **URL:** https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2022/11/30/2022-25998/endangered-and-threatened-wildlife-and-plants-endangered-species-status-for-northern-long-eared-bat
- **Jurisdiction:** Federal (all states)
- **Description:** Published rule in the Federal Register formally reclassifying the northern long-eared bat (Myotis septentrionalis) to endangered. Includes ESA section 7 consultation requirements and prohibition on incidental take.
- **Source:** Federal Register / USFWS

---

### 3. 4(d) Rule for the Northern Long-Eared Bat (2016, superseded but historically relevant)
- **URL:** https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2016/01/14/2016-00617/endangered-and-threatened-wildlife-and-plants-4d-rule-for-the-northern-long-eared-bat
- **Jurisdiction:** Federal (all states in NLEB range)
- **Description:** The original 4(d) special rule issued when the NLEB was listed as threatened, allowing limited incidental take under specific conditions. Now superseded by the 2023 endangered listing, but cited in many state guidance documents still in circulation.
- **Source:** Federal Register / USFWS

---

### 4. Federal Register — Proposed Endangered Species Status for Tricolored Bat (Sept 2022)
- **URL:** https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2022/09/14/2022-18852/endangered-and-threatened-wildlife-and-plants-endangered-species-status-for-tricolored-bat
- **Jurisdiction:** Federal (39 U.S. states including TX, TN, NC, GA, FL, OH, IN, MO, KS)
- **Description:** Proposed rule to list the tricolored bat (Perimyotis subflavus) as endangered under the ESA due to white-nose syndrome causing >90% population declines. Affects bat exclusion projects in all listed states.
- **Source:** Federal Register / USFWS

### 8. Migratory Bird Treaty Act of 1918 (Compiled Statute)
- **URL:** https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/COMPS-3009/pdf/COMPS-3009.pdf
- **Jurisdiction:** Federal (all states)
- **Description:** Full text of the MBTA as enacted and amended. Note: Bats are NOT covered by the MBTA (which applies only to birds). This document is included here to confirm bats fall outside MBTA protections and are instead regulated under the ESA and state nongame laws.

## Texas

---

### 9. Texas Parks and Wildlife Code — Chapter 63 (Nongame Animals)
- **URL:** https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/PW/htm/PW.63.htm
- **Jurisdiction:** Texas (statewide)
- **Description:** State statute governing nongame wildlife in Texas, including bats. Bats may not be hunted, killed, possessed, purchased, or sold. However, bats may be moved, trapped, or killed if inside or on a building occupied by people. Excluding bats from buildings is discouraged May 1–August 15.
- **Source:** Texas Legislature / Texas Capitol statutes

## Notes on Sources

- **Migratory Bird Treaty Act (MBTA):** Bats are NOT birds and are NOT protected by the MBTA. The MBTA governs migratory birds only. Bat protection at the federal level flows through the Endangered Species Act (for listed species) and state nongame wildlife laws.
- **Endangered Species Act (ESA):** Three bat species with direct relevance to AAAC service states currently hold federal ESA endangered status: **Indiana bat** (Myotis sodalis), **Northern long-eared bat** (Myotis septentrionalis, relisted as endangered March 2023), and **gray bat** (Myotis grisescens). The **tricolored bat** (Perimyotis subflavus) was proposed for endangered listing in September 2022 and a final rule was under review as of the research date.
- **Maternity Season Summary by State:**
  | State | Exclusion Prohibited | Source Authority |
  |-------|---------------------|-----------------|
  | TX | Discouraged May 1–Aug 15 | TPWD / Parks & Wildlife Code Ch. 63 |
  | TN | Prior TWRA approval required any time young present | TWRA Chapter 1660-1-21 |
  | NC | May 1–July 31 (moratorium) | NCWRC / G.S. 113-273 |
  | GA | Coordination with GADNR/USFWS required | GADNR / BPA Users' Guide |
  | FL | April 16–August 14 (prohibited by law) | FWC / FAC 68A-4.001 & 68A-9.010 |
  | OH | May 16–July 31 (5+ bats); Oct 15–Mar 31 (any bats) | ODNR / OAC 1501:31-15-03 |
  | IN | June–July primary restriction; inactive season Oct 1–Mar 31 | INDNR / INDOT guidance |
  | MO | Mid-May–mid-August (discouraged/restricted) | MDC / 3 CSR 10-4 |
  | KS | No specific state exclusion season law found; KDWP permit required | KDWP |
  | CO | No specific state exclusion season law found; CPW regulations apply | CPW / 2 CCR 406-10 |
  | WA | Mid-August–mid-October or early spring only | WDFW / WAC 232-12-011 |
