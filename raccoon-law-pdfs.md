## Texas

### 1. Fur-Bearing Animal Regulations Brochure
- **URL:** https://tpwd.texas.gov/publications/pwdpubs/media/pwd_br_w7000_0065.pdf
- **Source:** Texas Parks & Wildlife Department (TPWD)
- **Description:** Official TPWD publication covering fur-bearing animal regulations including raccoon trapping seasons, nuisance animal provisions, and who may trap without a license. Covers the authorization required for nuisance fur-bearer relocation.

### 2. TPWD Nuisance Fur-Bearing Animals (Web Regulation Page)
- **URL:** https://tpwd.texas.gov/regulations/outdoor-annual/hunting/fur-bearing-animal-regulations/nuisance-fur-bearing-animals
- **Source:** Texas Parks & Wildlife Department (TPWD)
- **Description:** Explains that raccoons may be captured and relocated only with written authorization from TPWD's Wildlife Division and landowner permission at the release site; monthly reporting is required. Relocation requires a specific letter of authorization.

### 3. Chapter 826 Rabies Control Rules — Texas Administrative Code (PDF)
- **URL:** https://www.dshs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/LIDS-Zoonosis/ACO/826rules14.pdf
- **Source:** Texas Department of State Health Services (DSHS)
- **Description:** Full text of the Texas Administrative Code rules implementing the Rabies Control Act. Explicitly prohibits transporting live raccoons indigenous or naturalized to North America (a Class C misdemeanor); exempts TPWD-authorized nuisance fur-bearer relocators.

### 4. Texas Health & Safety Code Chapter 826 — Rabies Control Act (PDF)
- **URL:** https://www.dshs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/IDCU/health/zoonosis/education/training/aco/manual/Information/826law17.pdf
- **Source:** Texas DSHS / Zoonosis Control
- **Description:** The enabling statute behind the administrative rules. Establishes raccoons as a high-risk rabies vector species; governs transport prohibitions and quarantine authority for wildlife suspected of rabies exposure.

### 5. Rabies (Basic) — Zoonosis Control Training Chapter (PDF)
- **URL:** https://www.dshs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/LIDS-Zoonosis/ACO/BasRabies-with-chart-23.pdf
- **Source:** Texas DSHS Zoonosis Control (Animal Control Officer Training)
- **Description:** Training document for animal control officers covering rabies vector species including raccoons, quarantine procedures, exposure handling, and public health obligations. Includes charts on species risk classification.

**Key Texas Rules:**
- Transporting live raccoons is **illegal** without TPWD nuisance fur-bearer relocation authorization (Class C misdemeanor under Health & Safety Code Ch. 826)
- Relocation requires: TPWD letter of authorization + landowner permission at release site + monthly activity reports
- Professional trappers charging fees must have appropriate TPWD permits

## Tennessee

### 6. TWRA Chapter 1660-1-21 — Nuisance Animal Damage Control Rules (PDF)
- **URL:** https://publications.tnsosfiles.com/rules/1660/1660-01/1660-01-21.pdf
- **Source:** Tennessee Secretary of State — Official TWRA Rules
- **Description:** Official administrative code chapter governing all nuisance wildlife damage control in Tennessee. Requires an Animal Damage Control Permit for anyone charging fees to trap/remove nuisance wildlife; establishes trap check intervals (36 hours for most traps) and handling requirements for raccoons.

### 7. TWRA Hunt Season Planner (PDF)
- **URL:** https://www.tn.gov/content/dam/tn/twra/documents/guide/TWRA-Hunt-Season-Planner.pdf
- **Source:** Tennessee Wildlife Resources Agency (TWRA)
- **Description:** Annual season planner including raccoon hunting and trapping seasons. Provides the legal season windows relevant to nuisance wildlife operators who rely on hunting/trapping season permissions.

### 8. TWRA Wildlife Possession Permit Application (PDF)
- **URL:** https://www.tn.gov/content/dam/tn/twra/documents/law-enforcement/Wildlife-WR-0548-Wildlife-Possession-Permit-Application-ClassII.pdf
- **Source:** Tennessee Wildlife Resources Agency (TWRA)
- **Description:** Application for possessing live wildlife including raccoons. Class II permit requirements apply to wildlife control operators holding raccoons prior to euthanasia or release.

**Key Tennessee Rules:**
- Relocation of raccoons is **illegal in some areas** due to rabies quarantine zones — operators must check with regional TWRA office
- Landowners/tenants may trap without a permit on their own property, but must comply with ADC permit rules if charging fees
- Trap check intervals: 36 hours for standard traps; 72 hours for completely submerged kill traps
- Raccoons are protected under state law; relocation requires TWRA permits where it is permitted at all

## North Carolina

- **URL:** https://www.ncwildlife.gov/media/2232/download?attachment=
- **Source:** North Carolina Wildlife Resources Commission (NCWRC)
- **Description:** Official guidance document stating that raccoons are a rabies vector species and **cannot be trapped and relocated under any circumstances** in NC. Trapped raccoons must be euthanized or released on the capture property. Covers legal methods of exclusion and hazing.

### 10. North Carolina Raccoon Wildlife Profile (PDF)
- **URL:** https://www.ncwildlife.gov/media/2231/download
- **Source:** North Carolina Wildlife Resources Commission (NCWRC)
- **Description:** Species profile covering raccoon biology, behavior, and the legal framework for managing nuisance raccoons in NC, including the rabies vector species designation and its legal implications for relocation.

### 11. Raccoons — NCWRC Extended Species Document (PDF)
- **URL:** https://www.ncwildlife.gov/media/2289/download?attachment=
- **Source:** North Carolina Wildlife Resources Commission (NCWRC)
- **Description:** Supplementary raccoon management document from NCWRC with details on regulations governing trapping, season dates, and the legal prohibition on relocation of raccoons as a rabies vector species.

### 12. NC Furbearer Newsletter — Spring/Summer 2025 (Web)
- **URL:** https://www.ncwildlife.gov/trapping/furbearer-newsletter-spring-summer-2025/open
- **Source:** North Carolina Wildlife Resources Commission (NCWRC)
- **Description:** Regulatory update effective March 1, 2025, extending 72-hour trap attendance to foothold and cage traps set as submersion trapping systems. Relevant to professional nuisance wildlife operators.

**Key North Carolina Rules:**
- Raccoon relocation is **prohibited under all circumstances** — raccoons are a designated rabies vector species
- Trapped raccoons must be euthanized or released on-site (the property of capture)
- Trapping license required; landowners trapping on their own property are exempt from the license requirement
- Daily trap inspection required for most trap types (72-hour rule applies only to fully submerged Conibear-type traps and, as of March 2025, submersion-set foothold/cage traps)

## Georgia

- **Source:** Georgia Department of Natural Resources — Wildlife Resources Division
- **Description:** Official permit information document. Explicitly states: "Animals may be killed or trapped and relocated (**no relocation of carnivores**)." Since raccoons are carnivores, relocation is prohibited. Covers permit duration (up to 90 days), species-specific issuance, and property-specific restrictions.

- **URL:** https://georgiawildlife.com/sites/default/files/wrd/pdf/uwp/UWP%20Raccoon%20Fact%20Sheet.pdf
- **Source:** Georgia DNR Wildlife Resources Division — Urban Wildlife Program
- **Description:** Public guidance PDF on managing nuisance raccoons; covers legal removal methods, when nuisance permits are required, and the prohibition on relocating carnivores including raccoons.

### 15. Georgia DNR Raccoon Fact Sheet — 2007 (PDF)
- **URL:** https://georgiawildlife.com/sites/default/files/wrd/pdf/fact-sheets/2007_raccoon.pdf
- **Source:** Georgia Department of Natural Resources Wildlife Resources Division
- **Description:** Background species and regulatory fact sheet covering raccoon biology and Georgia's legal framework for nuisance removal. Documents the carnivore relocation prohibition and season-based removal rules.

### 16. Wild Animal Rules — Georgia DNR Public Notice (PDF)
- **URL:** https://georgiawildlife.com/sites/default/files/Public%20Notice_Wild%20Animals.pdf
- **Source:** Georgia DNR (Subject 391-4-8)
- **Description:** Administrative rules governing wild animal possession, control, and removal in Georgia; includes species classifications relevant to nuisance wildlife operators.

**Key Georgia Rules:**
- Relocation of raccoons is **prohibited** — raccoons are carnivores and Georgia DNR explicitly bars carnivore relocation under nuisance wildlife control permits
- Raccoons may be removed by: hiring a permitted NWCO, removing during regulated hunting/trapping season, or obtaining a nuisance wildlife control permit (valid up to 90 days)
- Nuisance permit is property-specific and species-specific
- On private land: no closed season and no bag limit for raccoon and opossum hunting/trapping
- Contact: Georgia DNR Special Permits — https://gadnrle.org/special-permits#nuisance

## Florida

### 17. FWC Furbearer Trapping Regulations (Web)
- **URL:** https://myfwc.com/hunting/furbearers/
- **Source:** Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission (FWC)
- **Description:** Official FWC furbearer trapping page covering raccoon season dates, furbearer license requirements, and legal trap types. A furbearer license is required for anyone trapping raccoons with traps or snares.

### 18. FWC Nuisance Wildlife Permits (Web)
- **URL:** https://myfwc.com/license/wildlife/nuisance-wildlife-permits/
- **Source:** Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission (FWC)
- **Description:** Overview of all nuisance wildlife permits in Florida, including NWCO registration, gun-and-light permits, and steel trap permits for raccoons. Describes disposal and transport requirements — live-captured raccoons must be released legally or euthanized within 24 hours.

### 19. FWC Rule 68A-9.010 — Taking Nuisance Wildlife (Administrative Rule)
- **URL:** https://flrules.org/gateway/RuleNo.asp?ID=68A-9.010
- **Source:** Florida Administrative Code — FWC
- **Description:** The primary administrative rule governing nuisance wildlife removal in Florida. Restricts transport of live-trapped raccoons within, into, or from the state except by FWC permit; defines legal trap types; requires 24-hour trap inspection.

### 20. FWC Relocating Wildlife (Web)
- **URL:** https://myfwc.com/conservation/you-conserve/wildlife/relocate/
- **Source:** Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission (FWC)
- **Description:** Official FWC guidance on wildlife relocation. Raccoons may only be relocated within the county of capture, to a minimum of 40 contiguous acres, with written landowner permission at the release site, and only if no rabies alert or quarantine is in effect in the county.

**Key Florida Rules:**
- Relocation is **conditionally restricted**: within-county only, 40+ contiguous acres, written landowner permission, no active county rabies alert/quarantine
- Live-captured raccoons must be released legally or euthanized within **24 hours** of trap inspection
- Transporting live wild raccoons statewide is illegal without FWC permit or authorization
- Furbearer license required to trap raccoons with traps or snares
- NWCO registration required to provide trapping services for hire

## Ohio

### 21. Ohio Hunting and Trapping Regulations 2025-26 (PDF)
- **URL:** https://dam.assets.ohio.gov/image/upload/ohiodnr.gov/documents/wildlife/laws-regs-licenses/Ohio%20Hunting%20and%20Trapping%20Regulations%20ENGLISH.pdf
- **Source:** Ohio Department of Natural Resources (ODNR) — Division of Wildlife
- **Description:** Official annual regulations booklet covering raccoon trapping seasons (November 10 through January 31), no bag/possession limits, trap check requirements, and the nuisance wildlife provisions. Explicitly states it is **illegal to live trap and relocate** raccoons to a new area.

### 22. Ohio DNR — Nuisance Raccoons (Web)
- **URL:** https://ohiodnr.gov/discover-and-learn/land-water/issues-for-landowners/nuisance-raccoons
- **Source:** Ohio Department of Natural Resources (ODNR)
- **Description:** Official guidance confirming that nuisance raccoons may be trapped without a permit, but relocation is illegal. All live-trapped raccoons must be released on the homeowner's property or humanely euthanized. Covers disease concerns driving the relocation ban.

### 23. Ohio Revised Code Section 1531.40 — Nuisance Wild Animal Removal License (PDF)
- **URL:** https://codes.ohio.gov/assets/laws/revised-code/authenticated/15/1531/1531.40/12-29-2023/1531.40-12-29-2023.pdf
- **Source:** Ohio Legislature — Ohio Revised Code (official authenticated PDF)
- **Description:** Statutory requirement that no person shall provide nuisance wild animal removal or control services for hire without a license from the Chief of the Division of Wildlife. Governs licensing requirements for professional wildlife control operators in Ohio.

**Key Ohio Rules:**
- Raccoon relocation is **prohibited** — illegal to live trap and relocate to any new area
- Trapped raccoons must be released on-site or humanely euthanized
- Trapping without a permit is allowed for nuisance raccoons (no permit required)
- Professional NWCOs must hold a state nuisance wild animal removal license (ORC 1531.40)
- Trapping season: November 10 – January 31; no bag or possession limit

## Indiana

- **Source:** Indiana Department of Natural Resources (IDNR) — Fish & Wildlife
- **Description:** Official permit regulations for Indiana's Nuisance Wild Animal Control Permit program. Covers permit requirements, annual renewal by January 31, trap specifications, and the rule that raccoons must be euthanized or released within the county of capture on property where the releaser has permission.

### 25. Indiana Nuisance Wild Animal Control Permit Information (PDF)
- **URL:** https://www.in.gov/dnr/fish-and-wildlife/files/fw-NuisancePermitInfo.pdf
- **Source:** Indiana Department of Natural Resources (IDNR) — Fish & Wildlife
- **Description:** Companion information document explaining who needs a permit, testing requirements ($25 fee), which species are covered (including raccoons), and which species are federally protected and excluded. Relevant for Wildlife Control Operators operating for hire in Indiana.

- Landowners and tenants may trap/shoot raccoons on their own property **without a permit** if the raccoon is causing damage

- IDNR encourages humane euthanasia to prevent disease spread
- Anyone providing raccoon removal services for hire must hold a Nuisance Wild Animal Control Permit (requires passing a test and paying a $25 fee)
- Permit expires December 31 of the year issued; annual renewal required by January 31

### 28. MDC Raccoon Control (Web)
- **URL:** https://mdc.mo.gov/wildlife/nuisance-problem-species/raccoon-control
- **Source:** Missouri Department of Conservation (MDC)
- **Description:** Official MDC guidance stating that relocation "is not recommended because moving an animal can spread disease." Provides legal methods for controlling damage-causing raccoons, including out-of-season take without a permit.

- Trap requirements: smooth or rubber jaws; labeled with full name/address or Conservation Number; dens and nests may not be molested or destroyed

### 38. WDFW Furbearer Trapping Regulations 2022-2023 (PDF)
- **URL:** https://wdfw.wa.gov/sites/default/files/publications/02333/wdfw02333.pdf
- **Source:** Washington Department of Fish and Wildlife (WDFW)
- **Description:** Furbearer trapping regulations booklet including raccoon season dates, trap check requirements, and license requirements. A hunting or trapping license is required to hunt or trap raccoons during open season.

**Key Washington Rules:**
- Releasing wildlife **anywhere other than the property of capture** is unlawful without a WDFW permit

- Wildlife Control Operators (WCOs) may trap raccoons year-round under their certification

## Key Legal Themes Across All States

### Relocation Bans
Five of eleven states have **outright or near-outright relocation bans**: Ohio (prohibited), North Carolina (prohibited), Georgia (carnivore = prohibited), and Tennessee (prohibited in rabies areas, which covers most of the state). Florida and Washington require permits for any off-site release.

### Raccoon Roundworm (Baylisascaris procyonis)
All technicians handling raccoons should be aware of Baylisascaris — prevalence exceeds 80% in some regions. Relevant for worker safety protocols, PPE requirements, and client disclosures. CDC has documented severe pediatric neurological disease cases from raccoon latrine exposure (CDC MMWR 2025).

### Rabies Vector Species (RVS) Designation
Raccoons are the #1 terrestrial rabies vector in the US (29% of all wildlife rabies cases). This designation drives the relocation bans in NC, TN, OH, GA, and FL. Several states require RVS-specific handling protocols — including immediate euthanasia if the animal has had human or domestic animal contact.

### Professional Licensing
Every state requires some form of permit, license, or registration to provide wildlife removal services for hire. Requirements vary from simple annual licenses (Ohio, Georgia) to exam-based certifications (Kansas, Indiana). Florida and Washington require NWCO registration.

### Trap Check Intervals
- Most states: **24–36 hours**
- North Carolina/Tennessee: 36 hours standard; 72-hour exception for fully submerged Conibear/kill traps
- Florida: **24 hours** (stricter than most)
