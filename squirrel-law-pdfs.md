# Squirrel Laws, Regulations & Legal Requirements by State
*Compiled for AAAC Wildlife Removal operational states*
*Research date: March 2026*

---

## How to Use This Document

This document catalogs official government and university PDFs covering squirrel-related regulations in AAAC Wildlife Removal's operating states. It covers:
- Nuisance wildlife control operator (NWCO/ADC) permit requirements
- Trapping, relocation, and euthanasia rules
- Protected squirrel species and subspecies
- Hunting season regulations that apply to nuisance removal

**Key principle for operators:** In many states, squirrels are classified as game animals or furbearers — regulations written for hunters also apply to anyone trapping them, including nuisance operators.

## Texas

### PDF Documents

**1. Fur-bearing Animal Regulations — Texas Parks and Wildlife**
- **URL:** https://tpwd.texas.gov/publications/pwdpubs/media/pwd_br_w7000_0065.pdf
- **State:** Texas
- **Source:** Texas Parks & Wildlife Department (TPWD)
- **Description:** Official brochure covering all fur-bearing animal regulations including nuisance furbearers, trapping rules, licensing requirements, and relocation authorization procedures. Squirrels are classified as fur-bearing animals in Texas.

**2. Commercial Nongame Permit Regulations and Exceptions**
- **URL:** https://tpwd.texas.gov/publications/pwdpubs/media/pwd_br_w7000_0618.pdf
- **State:** Texas
- **Source:** Texas Parks & Wildlife Department (TPWD)
- **Description:** Covers nongame species permit requirements — relevant for flying squirrels, which are classified as nongame animals rather than furbearers in Texas.

### Key Regulatory Facts (Texas)

- **TPWD regulatory page (Nuisance Fur-bearing Animals):** https://tpwd.texas.gov/regulations/outdoor-annual/hunting/fur-bearing-animal-regulations/nuisance-fur-bearing-animals
- Squirrels are classified as **fur-bearing animals** in Texas
- No license required to trap squirrels causing damage to agricultural crops, livestock, poultry, or personal property — but carcasses/pelts **cannot** be possessed or sold
- **Relocation requires TPWD authorization:** A letter of authorization from the Wildlife Division is required, stating species and estimated numbers to be trapped
- Landowner permission is required before releasing any furbearing animal on a release property
- Monthly reporting is required when animals are trapped and relocated
- For professional/commercial nuisance control operators, a Fur-Bearing Propagation/Trapping permit is required: https://tpwd.texas.gov/business/permits/land/wildlife/furbearing/
- **Flying squirrels** fall under nongame regulations, not furbearer rules — see nongame protected species list

### Protected Species (Texas)
- No squirrel subspecies are currently state-listed as threatened in Texas
- Flying squirrels are nongame animals; possession of more than 25 specimens requires a commercial nongame permit
- Full protected species list: https://tpwd.texas.gov/huntwild/wild/rehab/protected/

- **Small Game Regulations page:** https://www.tn.gov/twra/hunting/small-game-regulations.html

### Key Regulatory Facts (North Carolina)

- **NCWRC regulations page:** https://www.ncwildlife.gov/hunting/fishing-hunting-trapping-regulations
- Squirrels cannot be legally trapped without a **Depredation Permit** from NCWRC (unless the animal is caught in the act of causing property damage — no permit needed in that specific scenario under NC Statute)

- It is unlawful to shoot at or into a squirrel's nest
- Professional nuisance operators need a Trapping License from NCWRC
- **Fox squirrel bag limit** of 1/day exists — fox squirrels are regulated game animals, not nuisance-exempt

### Protected Species (North Carolina)
- **Carolina Northern Flying Squirrel (*Glaucomys sabrinus coloratus*)** — **Federally Endangered** (listed under ESA 1985); found only above 4,500 feet elevation in western NC; absolutely no taking without federal permit
- Federal ECOS profile: https://ecos.fws.gov/ecp/species/2657
- Southern Fox Squirrel is a legal game animal in NC, not a protected subspecies

### Key Regulatory Facts (Georgia)

- **Georgia DNR Special Permits page:** https://gadnrle.org/special-permits#nuisance
- Nuisance control options: hire a licensed NWCO, remove the animal yourself during regulated hunting/trapping seasons, or obtain a **Nuisance Wildlife Control Permit** from GA DNR
- Permits last up to **90 days**, are property-specific and species-specific
- Permits **do NOT cover** deer, bear, turkey, waterfowl, or alligators — squirrels are eligible
- Relocation is allowed with **landowner permission at release site**; animals cannot be released on public lands without authorization; no relocation of carnivores (squirrels are not carnivores, so relocation is generally permitted)
- Permittee must maintain records (county, species, number, disposition, relocation site) and submit an annual report to DNR
- Permittee may possess nuisance wildlife for less than **72 hours** for transport and release
- No chemicals or poisons authorized under permit
- **Fox squirrel** is a legal game animal in Georgia; bag limit of 12/day gray squirrel, **1/day fox squirrel on WMAs**

### Protected Species (Georgia)
- No squirrel subspecies are currently state-listed as threatened or endangered in Georgia
- Fox squirrels are regulated game animals with bag limits but not listed as threatened

- **Description:** University extension publication covering all Florida squirrel species — gray, fox, Big Cypress fox, and flying squirrels — including identification and legal status notes.

### Key Regulatory Facts (Florida)

- **FWC Nuisance Wildlife Permits page:** https://myfwc.com/license/wildlife/nuisance-wildlife-permits/
- **FWC Removing Nuisance Wildlife page:** https://myfwc.com/conservation/you-conserve/wildlife/remove/
- FWC **does not license** nuisance wildlife trappers — Florida has no NWCO licensing program
- Landowners may take nuisance wildlife causing property damage or safety threats, or authorize another person to do so
- **Live-captured nuisance wildlife must be released legally or euthanized humanely within 24 hours** of capture or trap inspection
- Animals must be released on the **same contiguous property** where trapped, or transported only for euthanasia
- All traps must be inspected at least once every 24 hours
- Gray and red squirrels: lawful to trap as nuisance; must be released on-site or euthanized within 24 hours
- **Fox squirrels may not be taken or pursued** — all fox squirrel subspecies are protected from hunting throughout the state
- **Big Cypress Fox Squirrel** (*Sciurus niger avicennia*) — **State-Threatened** species; any intentional take requires a state permit for scientific or conservation purposes only
- Southern Fox Squirrel (formerly Sherman's) — removed from state list in 2018 but Species Conservation Measures still apply under 68A-29.002 F.A.C.
- Governing regulation: **Florida Administrative Code 68A-9.010** (nuisance wildlife)
- **Steel traps** require a separate FWC permit: https://myfwc.com/license/wildlife/nuisance-wildlife-permits/steel-traps/

### Protected Species (Florida)
- **All fox squirrel subspecies** — protected from hunting/pursuit throughout the state
- **Big Cypress Fox Squirrel (*S. n. avicennia*)** — State Threatened; no-take without permit
- Southern Fox Squirrel (*S. n. niger*, formerly Sherman's) — Species Conservation Measures still apply
- Eastern gray squirrel — no protected status; treatable as nuisance

- Only **live/cage-type or box traps** may be used — no body-gripping or kill traps for nuisance squirrel removal

  1. CPW is notified in advance
  2. Release site is appropriate habitat for the species
  3. Private landowner permission has been obtained at release site
  4. **Relocation is within 10 miles of capture site**

### Key Regulatory Facts (Washington)

- **WDFW Tree Squirrels page:** https://wdfw.wa.gov/species-habitats/living/species-facts/tree-squirrels
- **WDFW Nuisance Wildlife Trapping page:** https://wdfw.wa.gov/species-habitats/living/nuisance-wildlife/trapping
- **CRITICAL: It is unlawful to release a squirrel anywhere in Washington State other than on the property where it was legally trapped** — no relocation to other properties under any circumstances without a WDFW permit
- **Body-gripping traps are prohibited** for nuisance wildlife removal without a Special Trapping Permit from WDFW; only Conibear-type traps submerged in water, padded foothold traps, and non-strangling foot snares may be authorized
- Eastern gray squirrels are legal to trap and must either be released on the capture property or **euthanized** — relocation is prohibited
- Special Trapping Permits require demonstrating that non-lethal methods have been tried and have failed: https://wdfw.wa.gov/species-habitats/living/nuisance-wildlife/special-trapping
- Wildlife Control Operator (WCO) license is required for commercial operators: https://wdfw.wa.gov/species-habitats/living/nuisance-wildlife/wildlife-control-operators/faq
- WAC 232-12-011 lists all protected species in Washington

### Protected Species (Washington)
- **Western Gray Squirrel (*Sciurus griseus*)** — **State Endangered** (uplisted from Threatened by WA Fish and Wildlife Commission); **no take under any circumstances** without a specific scientific permit; WAC 220-200-100
- **Douglas' Squirrel (*Tamiasciurus douglasii*)** — State Protected; can only be taken in emergencies when damaging crops/domestic animals; requires special permit; RCW 77.36.030
- **Red Squirrel (*Tamiasciurus hudsonicus*)** — State Protected; same emergency-only taking rule applies; RCW 77.36.030
- **Northern Flying Squirrel (*Glaucomys sabrinus*)** — State Protected; same emergency-only taking rule; RCW 77.36.030
- **Eastern Gray Squirrel** — not native to WA; classified as wildlife but not protected — legal nuisance animal (but no relocation)
