# Platform Copy Protocols

Voice, tone, and structural patterns for LinkedIn and Instagram. Derived from frameworks in reference files, supplemented with current platform research.

**Important:** This file covers copy protocols (how to write), not technical specs (character limits, image dimensions). Technical specs change frequently. For current limits, consult official platform documentation before publishing.

## LINKEDIN

### Platform Philosophy

LinkedIn rewards professional credibility and genuine expertise. The audience is in "business mode" but still human. They want value they can apply, not corporate speak.

**Framework Derivations:**

| Principle | Source | Application |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Authority first | Cialdini (PSYCHOLOGY § 1.3) | Establish credentials early but naturally. Third-party validation works. |
| Reason-why depth | Hopkins (CLASSIC § 1.2) | Specific claims with concrete proof outperform vague assertions. |
| Professional reciprocity | Cialdini (PSYCHOLOGY § 1.1) | Lead with value before any ask. Teach, then offer. |
| Committee awareness | Bly (BRAND-B2B § 2.1) | Content may be shared with decision-makers. Write for multiple readers. |

### LinkedIn Voice Guidelines

**Do:**
- Write conversationally but professionally
- Use first person ("I learned" not "one learns")
- Include specific numbers, results, and timeframes
- Share genuine insights from experience
- Ask questions that invite professional discussion

**Don't:**
- Use corporate jargon (synergy, leverage, ecosystem)
- Post generic motivational content without substance
- Write walls of text without line breaks
- Sound like a press release
- Use hashtags excessively (3-5 max)

> **Research insight:** LinkedIn's algorithm prioritizes "professional knowledge and tips over content that merely seeks attention." Posts providing genuine expertise see higher distribution.
> 
> Source: LinkedIn Algorithm Insights 2024 Report, cited in https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/linkedin-best-practices-optimize-your-posts-according-xavier-degraux-gkfze

### LinkedIn Post Structure

**The Hook-Value-Engage Pattern:**

```
[LINE 1-2: Hook that earns the "see more" click]

[BODY: Value delivery - insight, lesson, or framework]

[CLOSE: Question or CTA that invites response]
```

**Hook Formulas (first 2 lines are critical):**

| Type | Formula | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Contrarian | "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's why:" | "Hustle culture is killing your productivity. Here's why:" |
| Credentialed insight | "After [X experience], I learned [unexpected lesson]" | "After 200 sales calls, I learned the pitch matters less than the pause." |
| Curiosity gap | "[Surprising result] happened when I [action]" | "Revenue doubled when I stopped doing demos." |
| Direct value | "[Number] [things] that [benefit]:" | "5 questions that close enterprise deals:" |

> **Research insight:** "The first line should grab attention and entice users to read more. After the second line, users will have to click the see more option."
>
> Source: https://copywritercollective.com/9-secrets-linkedin-copywriting/

### LinkedIn Format Performance

**Current effective formats (ranked by reach):**

| Format | Best For | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Carousel/PDF | Educational content, how-to guides | Aim for ~12 slides. Under 500 characters in accompanying text. |
| Multi-image | Narratives, case studies | Higher engagement than single images |
| Text-only | Personal branding, thought leadership | Works well for personal profiles, less for company pages |
| Video | Process explanations, demonstrations | Best between 11am-1pm, Tuesdays/Thursdays |

> **Research insight:** "Using the same format repeatedly can reduce the reach of your posts by 30%." Vary your format mix.
>
> Source: Algorithm Insights 2024 Report, cited in https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/linkedin-best-practices-optimize-your-posts-according-xavier-degraux-gkfze

### LinkedIn Document/Carousel Structure

**Framework application:** Apply Sugarman's Slippery Slide (PSYCHOLOGY § 3.1). Each slide earns the next swipe.

```
SLIDE 1: Hook headline (bold claim or question)
SLIDE 2: Problem or context setup
SLIDES 3-10: One point per slide, building value
SLIDE 11-12: Summary + CTA
```

**Carousel copy rules:**
- One idea per slide maximum
- Large, readable text
- Educational/how-to content performs best
- Avoid: promotional content, pure inspiration without tactics

> **Research insight:** "Educational and useful carousels, such as 'how-to' guides, work the best. Product/service presentations see -60 to -70% effectiveness."
>
> Source: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/linkedin-best-practices-optimize-your-posts-according-xavier-degraux-gkfze

### LinkedIn Engagement Tactics

**Derived from Cialdini's principles:**

| Trigger | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Social proof | "Join 10,000+ professionals who..." / "Used by teams at [logos]" |
| Authority | Cite specific experience: "Over 15 years in [field]..." |
| Consistency | Reference past posts: "Last week I shared X. Here's the follow-up..." |
| Liking | Find genuine similarities with audience. Mirror their language. |

> **Research insight:** "Posts with images typically see a 2x higher comment rate versus those without. Companies that post weekly see a 2x lift in engagement."
>
> Source: https://www.linkedin.com/business/marketing/blog/linkedin-pages/5-non-obvious-ways-to-improve-your-linkedin-company-page

## INSTAGRAM

### Platform Philosophy

Instagram is visual-first, emotion-driven, and fast-scrolling. The caption supports the visual, not the other way around. Authenticity and relatability outperform polish.

**Framework Derivations:**

| Principle | Source | Application |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Slippery slide | Sugarman (PSYCHOLOGY § 3.1) | First line must earn the "more" tap. Then each line earns the next. |
| Hook-Story-Offer | Brunson (MODERN-DIGITAL § 2.1) | Carousels especially benefit from this narrative arc. |
| Emotional resonance | Kahneman System 1 (PSYCHOLOGY § 2.3) | Visual + emotional caption beats logical argument. |
| Unity/belonging | Cialdini (PSYCHOLOGY § 1.7) | Community language ("we," insider references) builds connection. |

### Instagram Voice Guidelines

**Do:**
- Write like texting a friend
- Use emojis strategically (not excessively)
- Be vulnerable and authentic
- Include personality and opinion
- Match energy to the visual

**Don't:**
- Write corporate-sounding copy
- Use all caps for entire captions
- Stuff hashtags into the caption body (put at end or in comments)
- Ignore the visual (caption should complement, not repeat)
- Be generic (specificity wins)

> **Research insight:** "Captions containing an emoji have a 15% higher interaction rate on the platform."
>
> Source: https://www.sendible.com/insights/how-to-write-instagram-captions

### Instagram Caption Structure

**The ABCs of Instagram Copy:**

```
A - ATTENTION: Hook in first line (before the "more" truncation)
B - BENEFIT: Value through education, entertainment, or inspiration
C - CLOSE: Clear call-to-action
```

> **Research insight:** "Captions between 65-75 words (400 characters) get the highest engagement on Instagram."
>
> Source: https://pallyy.com/blog/instagram-captions

**Caption Length Strategy:**

| Length | Use For | Percentage of Posts |
|--------|---------|---------------------|
| Short (<150 chars) | Memes, quick engagement, Reels | 60% |
| Medium (150-300 words) | Storytelling, brand building | 30% |
| Long (700-2,200 chars) | Educational content, case studies | 10% |

> **Research insight:** "Short captions (less than 30 words or 120-150 characters) tend to have the highest engagement rate across different post types."
>
> Source: Socialinsider study, cited in https://www.shopify.com/blog/instagram-captions

### Instagram Carousel Structure

**Framework application:** Apply AIDA (Schwartz awareness progression adapted) across slides.

```
SLIDE 1: ATTENTION - Bold hook that stops the scroll
SLIDE 2-3: INTEREST - Name the problem, build context
SLIDE 4-7: DESIRE/DETAIL - Deliver the value (one point per slide)
SLIDE 8-10: ACTION - Summary, CTA, next step
```

**Carousel copy rules:**
- First slide is everything (second chance if they skip: algorithm reshows slide 2)
- One idea per slide, large readable text
- Build curiosity that rewards swiping
- End with clear CTA

> **Research insight:** "Instagram carousels see an average engagement rate of around 10%. That's a huge jump compared to single photos (around 7%) and Reels (around 6%)."
>
> Source: https://www.cometly.com/post/instagram-carousel-post

> **Research insight:** "Strong hooks, simple takeaways, and consistent formatting keep people swiping."
>
> Source: https://blog.hootsuite.com/instagram-carousel/

### Instagram Carousel Types That Work

| Type | Structure | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Step-by-step tutorial | Problem → Steps → Result | Educational content |
| Before/after | Transformation story | Results showcase |
| Tips list | Hook → Tip per slide → Summary | Quick value delivery |
| Story arc | Setup → Conflict → Resolution → Lesson | Personal brand building |
| Product showcase | Hero shot → Features → Benefits → CTA | E-commerce |

> **Research insight:** "Before and after comparisons drive 5.13% higher engagement than single-image posts."
>
> Source: https://panocollages.com/blog/10-instagram-carousel-templates-that-drive-engagement

### Instagram Hook Formulas

**First line must earn the "...more" tap:**

| Type | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Bold claim | "This changed everything about [topic]." |
| Question | "Why does [common thing] always [frustrating result]?" |
| Contrarian | "Stop [common advice]. Here's what actually works." |
| Story entry | "[Time marker], I was [relatable struggle]..." |
| Direct value | "[Number] [things] you need to know about [topic]:" |

### Instagram CTA Patterns

**Derived from Cialdini's consistency principle:** Small asks build to larger ones.

| CTA Type | Example | When to Use |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Engagement | "Double tap if you agree" | Building algorithm signals |
| Comment prompt | "Drop a 🔥 if this resonates" | Increasing comments |
| Save prompt | "Save this for later" | Valuable reference content |
| Share prompt | "Tag someone who needs this" | Expanding reach |
| Conversion | "Link in bio for the full guide" | Driving traffic |

> **Research insight:** "Guide your customers towards activities that drive business impact. Ask them to tag their friends in the comments to increase reach, or direct them to the link in your bio."
>
> Source: https://sproutsocial.com/insights/instagram-caption-ideas/

### Instagram Hashtag Strategy

**Current best practice:** Quality over quantity. Mix of reach levels.

| Category | Purpose | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Broad (1M+ posts) | Reach | #marketing |
| Medium (100K-1M) | Discovery | #contentmarketingtips |
| Niche (<100K) | Targeted audience | #b2bcontentwriter |

- Use 5-10 targeted hashtags (not 30)
- Place at end of caption or in first comment
- Research hashtags for your specific niche

> **Research insight:** "Use 5-10 targeted hashtags. Instagram penalizes spammy overuse."
>
> Source: https://www.hireawriter.us/social/instagram-captions-best-practices-for-2025

## Cross-Platform Principles

These frameworks apply regardless of platform:

### From Sugarman (PSYCHOLOGY § 3.1-3.3)
- **Slippery slide:** Every element earns the next
- **Curiosity seeds:** Plant open loops that must be resolved
- **Specificity:** Concrete details beat vague claims

### From Cialdini (PSYCHOLOGY § 1.1-1.7)
- **Reciprocity:** Give value before asking
- **Social proof:** Show others like them succeeding
- **Authority:** Establish credibility naturally
- **Scarcity:** Real limits create urgency (don't fake it)

### From Ogilvy (BRAND-B2B § 1.3-1.4)
- **Headline investment:** First line is 80 cents of your dollar
- **Specificity over superlatives:** "3.3x better" beats "much better"
- **Long copy when warranted:** Length doesn't bore; bad copy does

### From Schwartz (CLASSIC § 2.1)
- **Meet awareness level:** Don't pitch product to problem-unaware audiences
- **Channel existing desire:** Connect to what they already want
